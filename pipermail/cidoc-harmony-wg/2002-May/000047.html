Thanks, Martin, for the pointer. You can also give a look at the paper

Oltramari, A., Gangemi A., Guarino N., Masolo C. 2002. Restructuring 
WordNet's Top-Level: The OntoClean approach. To appear in Proceedings 
of LREC2002 (OntoLex workshop). Las Palmas, Spain, retrievable from

http://www.ladseb.pd.cnr.it/infor/Ontology/Papers/Oltramari2.pdf

A polished version of this paper has been accepted for publication on 
AI Magazine (fall issue), and should be ready in 2 days.

An axiomatized version of what has been called "The OCT ontology" 
(but we are not sure of the name yet) is expected to be delivered by 
the end of June, as part of the WonderWeb project 
(http://wonderweb.semanticweb.org). By that time, we shall also make 
available a Loom implementation, with links to WordNet.

The relationships with WordNet and other lexical resources will be 
discussed at the OntoLex workshop starting next week, see 
http://www.ontotext.com/ontolex. In particular, we are looking 
forward to have  some fruitful interaction with the WordNet 
developers (Christiane Fellbaum), who already gave us very positive 
feedbacks.

I am planning to work with Martin on the results of your meeting at 
the ISWC conference in Sardinia, just the week after the Crete 
meeting.

Cheers,

Nicola
