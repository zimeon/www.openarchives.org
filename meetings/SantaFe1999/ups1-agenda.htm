Agenda for the Santa Fe meeting of the Open Archives initiative, October 21-22 1999

__________________

The results we hope to come out of the meeting:

  • Comments on a proposal for an extendible framework in which providers of data and implementors of data describe how data can be used / is being used. An insight in the intentions of the Santa Fe agreement proposal is given in the SFA Summary.
  • Initiation of project(s) to build cross-archive end-user service(s) - Identification of strategies to find funding.
  • Recommendations for archive solutions regarding aspects that will enable them to contribute their data in cross-archive end-user services.
  • Proposals for future initiatives to promote archive-based communication.
________________

Agenda

October 21 1999 Moderators throughout the meeting will be Clifford Lynch and Don Waters.
Welcome 08:30 - 08:40 Opening address by Paul Ginsparg
Part a   The creation of end-user services for distributed archives with hybrid architectures.

The problem statement to be addressed here starts from the consideration that, realistically, there will be many different types of archives with very different architectures etc... The aim is to create end-user services based on - some of - the data of - some of - these distributed and dissimilar archives. One example of such a cross-archive service is a multidisciplinary search facility. But one could think of many other criteria that services could use to derive their identity from.

    • Ed Fox

Part b   Institutional and Discipline-oriented archive solutions.

The aim is to try and identify the pros and cons of Institutional and Discipline-oriented archives and their impact on the overall goal of promoting archive solutions. This discussion can touch on a broad range of technical, organizational, political, ... issues.

Part c   Metadata.

The aim is to try and identify realistic guidelines regarding metadata requirements for archive solutions, in order to enable the archives to contribute data to cross-archive end-user services.

October 22 1999  
Part d   Interoperability, general.

The aim is to discuss which level of interoperability can/should be established inbetween archive solutions and between archives and established communication mechanisms. This discussion can depart from findings of October 21 and/or can introduce new elements. 

08:30 - 10:30 Group discussion

The aim is to try and formulate concrete projects and to try and identify strategies to find funding.

Part f   Strategies for the promotion of archive solutions.

The aim is to discuss and try to identify strategies that can lead to the further promotion of the use of archive solutions as a tool to optimize scholarly communication.
